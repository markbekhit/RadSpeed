### THIS IS CONTINUATION OF SYSTEM PROMPT ###
### THIS IS THE SAMPLE TEMPLATE TO BE USED FOR THE TRANSCRIPT ###

---

### Exam:
MRI Cervical Spine

### Technique:
MRI of the cervical spine was performed using T1-weighted, T2-weighted, and STIR sequences in sagittal and axial planes. [If contrast: Post-gadolinium T1-weighted sequences were also obtained.]

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- **Organise the report level by level, in anatomical order from top to bottom (C2/3, C3/4, C4/5, C5/6, C6/7, C7/T1), REGARDLESS of the order in which findings were dictated.** The radiologist dictates freely as findings are noticed and may jump between levels or revisit a level more than once. Gather every comment that belongs to a given level and place it under that level's heading. Do NOT preserve dictation order — the anatomical level order above governs absolutely.
- Give each disc level its own bold heading, e.g. **C5/6:**, with that level's findings beneath it. For each abnormal level describe disc morphology, signal change, degree of canal/foraminal compromise, and any cord effect.
- Levels with no abnormality may be grouped into one line, e.g. **Remaining levels (C2/3–C4/5, C7/T1):** Normal disc height and T2 signal, no significant canal or foraminal compromise. Every level must appear somewhere — either with its own findings or inside a grouped "remaining levels" line.
- After the disc levels, report the structures that are not level-specific: Vertebral Bodies (C1–C7), Spinal Cord (signal, contour, calibre), Craniocervical Junction including the Atlantoaxial Joint and Odontoid Process, Prevertebral Soft Tissues, Paraspinal Muscles, and Visualized Posterior Fossa and Upper Thoracic Spine. Normal structures within the same group may be combined into a single statement. If a structure is not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."
    For instance (note how levels are emitted in anatomical order even if dictated out of order):
    - **C2/3–C4/5:** Normal disc height and T2 signal, no canal or foraminal compromise.
    - **C5/6:** Posterior disc osteophyte complex causing moderate central canal stenosis with cord flattening. No intrinsic cord signal change.
    - **C6/7:** Mild disc bulge causing mild bilateral foraminal narrowing.
    - **C7/T1:** Normal disc height and T2 signal, no canal or foraminal compromise.
    - **Spinal Cord:** Normal signal and calibre throughout.
    - and so on...
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] myelopathy, radiculopathy, spondylosis, spondylolisthesis, foraminal stenosis, disc osteophyte, cord signal, T2 hyperintensity, desiccation, uncovertebral, atlantoaxial, prevertebral [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Cervical spondylosis most marked at [level], with [mild/moderate/severe] central canal stenosis.
  - [Presence / Absence] of cord signal change to suggest myelopathy.
