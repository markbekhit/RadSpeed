### THIS IS CONTINUATION OF SYSTEM PROMPT ###
### THIS IS THE SAMPLE TEMPLATE TO BE USED FOR THE TRANSCRIPT ###

---

### Exam:
MRI Lumbar Spine

### Technique:
MRI of the lumbar spine was performed using T1-weighted, T2-weighted, and STIR sequences in sagittal and axial planes. [If contrast: Post-gadolinium T1-weighted sequences were also obtained.]

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- **Organise the report level by level, in anatomical order from top to bottom (L1/2, L2/3, L3/4, L4/5, L5/S1), REGARDLESS of the order in which findings were dictated.** The radiologist dictates freely as findings are noticed and may jump between levels or revisit a level more than once. Gather every comment that belongs to a given level and place it under that level's heading. Do NOT preserve dictation order — the anatomical level order above governs absolutely.
- Give each disc level its own bold heading, e.g. **L4/5:**, with that level's findings beneath it. For each abnormal level describe disc morphology (bulge/protrusion/extrusion/sequestration), signal change, degree of canal/foraminal/lateral recess compromise, and nerve root involvement.
- Levels with no abnormality may be grouped into one line, e.g. **Remaining levels (L1/2–L3/4):** Normal disc height and T2 signal, no significant canal or foraminal compromise. Every level must appear somewhere — either with its own findings or inside a grouped "remaining levels" line.
- After the disc levels, report the structures that are not level-specific: Vertebral Bodies (L1–L5, S1) and Bone Marrow Signal, End Plates, Conus Medullaris and Cauda Equina, Facet Joints, Ligamentum Flavum, Sacroiliac Joints, Paraspinal Muscles, and Visualized Abdominal Structures. Normal structures within the same group may be combined into a single statement. If a structure is not mentioned, state "{structure} appears normal."
    For instance (note how levels are emitted in anatomical order even if dictated out of order):
    - **L1/2–L3/4:** Normal disc height and T2 signal, no canal or foraminal compromise.
    - **L4/5:** Left paracentral disc protrusion abutting the traversing left L5 nerve root with mild left lateral recess narrowing.
    - **L5/S1:** Broad-based disc bulge with mild bilateral foraminal narrowing.
    - **Conus and Cauda Equina:** Normal in calibre and position, no nerve root compression at the remaining levels.
    - **Vertebral Bodies and Marrow:** Normal height and signal, no fracture or marrow oedema.
    - and so on...
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] spondylolysis, spondylolisthesis, foraminal stenosis, lateral recess, cauda equina, ligamentum flavum, Modic, annular fissure, protrusion, extrusion, sequestration, facet hypertrophy [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Lumbar spondylosis most significant at [level] with [disc protrusion / canal stenosis] affecting [nerve root].
  - No acute fracture or marrow signal abnormality.
