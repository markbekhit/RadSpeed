### Exam:
CT Lumbar Spine

### Technique:
Axial cuts were obtained from the T12/L1 junction to the sacrum. Sagittal and coronal reformatting were then obtained. Bone and soft tissue window reconstructions were reviewed.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- **Organise the level-specific findings level by level, in anatomical order from top to bottom (L1/2, L2/3, L3/4, L4/5, L5/S1), REGARDLESS of the order in which they were dictated.** The radiologist dictates freely as findings are noticed and may jump between levels or revisit a level more than once. Gather every comment that belongs to a given level and place it under that level's heading. Do NOT preserve dictation order — the anatomical level order above governs absolutely.
- Give each abnormal disc level its own bold heading, e.g. **L4/5:**, describing disc-space and osteophyte morphology, facet and foraminal changes, and any neural compromise. Levels with no abnormality may be grouped, e.g. **Remaining levels (L1/2–L3/4):** No significant disc space narrowing or foraminal compromise. Every level must appear somewhere.
- After the levels, report the structures that are not level-specific: Vertebral Bodies (L1–L5, S1), Pedicles and Posterior Elements, Sacrum and Sacroiliac Joints, Paraspinal Muscles, and Visualized Abdominal Structures. Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement; only itemise individually when pathology is present. If not mentioned in the transcript, state "{structure} appears normal."
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] spondylosis, spondylolisthesis, spondylolysis, foraminal stenosis, lateral recess, cauda equina, annular fissure, facet hypertrophy, ligamentum flavum, disc desiccation, laminectomy [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Lumbar spondylosis with [level-specific] disc disease causing [mild/moderate/severe] canal/foraminal narrowing.
  - *No acute fracture identified.*
