### Exam:
CT Cervical Spine

### Technique:
Axial cuts were obtained from the skull base to the cervicothoracic junction. Sagittal and coronal reformatting were then obtained. Bone and soft tissue window reconstructions were reviewed.

### Clinical Details
**Instructions:**
- Record clinical details exactly as dictated. Do not rephrase or reformulate the wording.
- **Exclude** patient name, date of birth, age, gender, and MRN/accession numbers from the report, even if mentioned in the transcript.
- **If not provided:** 'No clinical details provided.'

### Comparison:
**Instructions:**
- **If provided:** List the prior imaging studies mentioned (modality and date/year if stated). Do not summarise changes here — comparison findings belong in the Findings section.
- **If not provided:** 'No prior imaging available for comparison.'

### Findings:
**Instructions:**
- **Organise the level-specific findings level by level, in anatomical order from top to bottom (C2/3, C3/4, C4/5, C5/6, C6/7, C7/T1), REGARDLESS of the order in which they were dictated.** The radiologist dictates freely as findings are noticed and may jump between levels or revisit a level more than once. Gather every comment that belongs to a given level and place it under that level's heading. Do NOT preserve dictation order — the anatomical level order above governs absolutely.
- Give each abnormal disc level its own bold heading, e.g. **C5/6:**, describing disc-space and osteophyte morphology, uncovertebral and facet changes, foraminal narrowing, and any neural compromise. Levels with no abnormality may be grouped, e.g. **Remaining levels (C2/3–C4/5, C7/T1):** No significant disc space narrowing or foraminal compromise. Every level must appear somewhere.
- After the levels, report the structures that are not level-specific: Vertebral Bodies (C1–C7), Pedicles and Posterior Elements, Craniocervical Junction including the Atlantoaxial Joint and Odontoid Process, Prevertebral Soft Tissues, and Visualized Posterior Fossa and Cervicothoracic Junction. Normal structures within the same anatomical group may be combined into a single statement; only itemise individually when pathology is present. If the transcript doesn't mention a certain structure or level, state that it "appears normal."
- **Commonly Misspelled Words:**
[correct spellings] osteophyte, spondylosis, spondylolisthesis, foraminal, myelopathy, radiculopathy, uncovertebral, facet, listhesis, odontoid, atlantoaxial, prevertebral [correct spellings]
- If 'query' is mentioned in the transcript, it should be rendered as '?' in the report.

### Impression:
**Instructions:**
- Create an impression from findings, into a brief description in bullet points.
- Include only clinically relevant findings. Minor incidental findings that are fully described in the body do not need to be restated here.
- For a normal/negative study, include a closing statement where appropriate, e.g. 'No cause for the patient's symptoms identified on this examination.'
- **Sample:**
  - Cervical spondylosis with [level-specific] disc osteophyte complex causing [mild/moderate/severe] foraminal narrowing.
  - *No acute fracture or subluxation identified.*
